DIY printing offers affordable custom gear solutions for teams, clubs, and businesses. Unlike commercial services, you maintain control over high-quality apparel customization, order custom tees online with ease, and create personalized products for giveaways or events.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Pick a Niche

Choose teams, clubs, businesses, or events to maximize your custom apparel sales.

2. Design Your Own Tee

Create graphics for personalized merchandise. Use Canva or Photoshop. Start with simple designs, expand to multi-color outfits.

3. Prepare Kits

Use beginner-friendly kits to avoid large commercial machines. Emulsion, screens, and ink are included.

4. Print Clothing

Test on scrap fabrics first, then print hoodies, jackets, tumblers, and other custom gear.

5. Cure Designs

Heat press or flash dryer ensures durable designs on apparel and promotional items.

6. Scale Business

Offer bulk custom wear, custom merchandise for events, and personalized goods for giveaways.

FAQ

  • Q: Can beginners start at home?
    A: Yes, kits and tutorials allow anyone to create personalized merchandise.
  • Q: Initial investment?
    A: Starter kits are under $200 for custom gear.
  • Q: Where to sell?
    A: Online, local orders, or corporate giveaways.
  • Q: Time to learn?
    A: 1–2 days to produce first items.
  • Q: Recurring costs?
    A: Only supplies like ink, fabric, and accessories.
  • Q: Which fabrics?
    A: Cotton and blends work best for personalized clothing.
  • Q: Scale business?
    A: Gradually increase batch sizes and event merchandise.
  • Q: Eco-friendly?
    A: Water-based inks and reusable screens reduce impact.
  • Q: Multi-color designs?
    A: Separate screens for each color, registration marks recommended.
  • Q: Profit potential?
    A: $10–$30 per custom shirt depending on markup and volume.
