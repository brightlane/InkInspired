Step 1: Choose Your Printing Method

🖥️ Screen Printing

Best for bold designs and bulk orders. Uses screens, ink, and a squeegee. Higher upfront learning curve, but the highest quality and profit margin.

🔥 Heat Transfer / HTV

Great for beginners. Cut vinyl designs and press them with a heat press. Works on shirts, bags, hats, and more.

🖨️ Sublimation

Best for polyester fabrics and mugs. Full-color photo-quality prints. Requires sublimation-coated blanks.

✍️ DTG (Direct to Garment)

Inkjet-style printing directly on fabric. Great for complex designs, but machines are expensive — not ideal for pure beginners.

Step 2: Pick Your Niche

Don't try to sell to everyone. Pick one audience to start:

  • Local sports teams needing jerseys and practice gear
  • Small businesses wanting branded uniforms
  • Schools and clubs needing event shirts
  • Bachelorette/bachelor parties (huge demand!)
  • Holiday and seasonal gifts
  • Pet owners wanting custom pet apparel

Step 3: Design Your Artwork

Free and paid tools to create printable designs:

  • Canva — free, beginner-friendly, great for text-based designs
  • Adobe Express — free tier, professional templates
  • Inkscape — free, vector graphics (best for screen printing)
  • Affinity Designer — one-time purchase, professional grade

Tip: Screen printing works best with vector files (SVG, AI, EPS). For heat transfer, PNG with transparent background is ideal.

Step 4: Order Your Kit and Practice

Beginner kits include everything: screens, squeegees, emulsion, ink, and instructions. Practice on scrap fabric before printing on actual product.

Step 5: Cure and Quality-Check

Curing sets the ink so it lasts through washing. Options:

  • Heat press — most consistent, recommended
  • Flash dryer — faster for production batches
  • Household oven — works for small batches (use separate oven)
  • Clothes dryer — budget option, less reliable

Always wash test one shirt before fulfilling a bulk order.

Step 6: Price, List, and Sell

Rule of thumb: price at 2.5–4× your material cost. Include your time. Sell on Etsy, local Facebook groups, or your own website.
