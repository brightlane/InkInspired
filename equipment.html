Equipment Guide: What You Actually Need

The internet will tell you that you need thousands of dollars of gear. You don't — at least not to start. Here's the honest breakdown.

Beginner Kit (Start Here)

A good beginner kit includes:

  • ✅ Silk screen frame(s)
  • ✅ Photo emulsion + sensitizer
  • ✅ Squeegee
  • ✅ Plastisol or water-based inks
  • ✅ Transparency sheets for film positives
  • ✅ Step-by-step tutorial

Cost: $50–$150 for a solid beginner kit vs $5,000+ for commercial equipment.

Optional Upgrades (Add Later)

  • Heat Press ($200–$400) — for consistent curing and HTV projects
  • Flash Dryer ($150–$300) — speeds up production between colors
  • Exposure Unit ($200–$600) — consistent screen burning vs sun exposure
  • Multi-color press ($800+) — for 2–6 color designs at scale

Start with a kit. Upgrade only when orders justify the investment.

Fabrics That Print Best

  • 🥇 100% Cotton — best ink absorption, most beginner-friendly
  • 🥈 50/50 Cotton-Poly blend — softer feel, slightly less vivid
  • 🥉 100% Polyester — needs sublimation or special ink
  • ⚠️ Dark fabrics — require white underbase layer first
